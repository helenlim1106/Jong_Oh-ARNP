An Advanced Registered Nurse Practitioner (ARNP) is more than “just a nurse.” ARNPs are elite, autonomous healthcare providers who have completed at least six years of rigorous post-secondary and graduate education. They begin their careers as registered nurses, then advance through master’s- or doctoral-level training in clinical assessment, diagnostic reasoning, and evidence-based treatment within their specialty.

After this intensive coursework, ARNPs must pass a national certification exam and obtain state licensure—credentials that grant them full prescriptive authority in every U.S. state. ARNPs can independently order and interpret laboratory or imaging studies, develop and manage treatment plans, prescribe medications, and bill insurance companies just like physicians.
